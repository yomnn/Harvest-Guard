Light
Light is crucial for photosynthesis, the process by which plants convert sunlight into energy. Different plants have varying light requirements, and factors such as intensity, duration, and quality of light can affect their growth.
Water
Water is essential for plant survival and is involved in nutrient transport, photosynthesis, and structural support. Too much or too little water can be detrimental; water availability varies depending on the plant species and environmental conditions.
Temperature
Temperature affects plant metabolism, growth, and development. Different plants have specific temperature ranges for optimal growth, and extreme temperatures (hot or cold) can stress plants.
Fertilizers
Fertilizers are crucial for plants as they supply essential nutrients, including nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium, necessary for growth and development. They compensate for soil deficiencies, promote increased crop yield, enhance flowering and fruit production, and help plants recover from stress conditions.
The soil
Soil provides plants with essential nutrients, anchorage, and a medium for root growth. Soil composition, pH, and drainage can impact plant health and different plants thrive in different types of soil.
Wind
Wind can influence the structure and growth of plants. Strong winds may cause damage, particularly to tall or delicate plants. Wind can also play a role in seed dispersal and pollen movement.
